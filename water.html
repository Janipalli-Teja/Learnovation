Water level indicator

Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Gather the Components

Make sure you have all the required components: Arduino Uno board, water level sensor (e.g., ultrasonic sensor or float sensor), breadboard, jumper wires, a buzzer, red LED, green LED, and yellow LED.

Step 2: Connect the Components

  • Connect the VCC and GND pins of the water level sensor to the 5V and GND pins of the Arduino Uno, respectively.
  • Connect the signal pin of the water level sensor to a digital pin on the Arduino Uno (e.g., pin 7).
  • Connect the red LED's anode (longer leg) to pin 9 on the Arduino and the cathode (shorter leg) to GND through a current-limiting resistor (220 ohms).
  • Connect the green LED's anode to pin 10 on the Arduino and the cathode to GND through a current-limiting resistor (220 ohms).
  • Connect the yellow LED's anode to pin 11 on the Arduino and the cathode to GND through a current-limiting resistor (220 ohms).
  • Connect the positive (anode) of the buzzer to pin 12 on the Arduino and the negative (cathode) to GND.

Step 4: Test the Water Level Indicator

the water level sensor detects water, the red LED and buzzer will turn on, indicating a high water level. When there is no water detected, the green and yellow LEDs will be on, indicating a low water level.

Note: The code provided is a basic example and assumes that the water level sensor output is connected to a digital pin and gives a HIGH signal when water is detected. Depending on the water level sensor you use, you may need to adjust the code accordingly.
